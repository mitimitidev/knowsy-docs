Product vision

Knowsy — "Carfax for homes"

Knowsy is a home-buying research platform that aggregates public data to create comprehensive property reports for homebuyers. The concept is similar to Carfax for homes — providing detailed information before making an offer, not after closing.

The platform aims to be the single source for all neighborhood and property information, eliminating the need to leave the platform.

Target market

Millennial / zillennial homebuyers who want a modern, engaging UI rather than sterile, investor-focused reports.

Differentiation

Consumer-focused design and a storytelling approach to data presentation — not a spreadsheet of metrics.

Pricing

$40 per report. Stripe collects the email at checkout — no separate sign-up form needed.

Positioning

Not competing with property-search platforms like Zillow — this is a post-selection verification tool, used once a buyer has a property in mind.

What a report contains

Data categories

Property summary Transactional data Owner history Town information Historical documents

Public data sources

Tax history School ratings Walkability scores Water quality (EPA) HOA documents Demographic data Political orientation Flood zones (FEMA) Social sentiment (Reddit) Top / worst restaurants & parks Noise pollution (HowLoud) Town meeting minutes

Historical deed documents

Historical deed documents dating back to 1913 are scanned using AI to identify potential issues like shared driveways, utility easements, or estate ownership that could complicate closing. County assessment rolls provide neighbor information — for example, the Westchester County assessment roll is 64,000 pages.

Key product decisions

  • Report page lives at a unique, unguessable URL (no login required to view).
  • Stripe collects email at checkout — no separate sign-up form needed.
  • Confirmation page can poll for readiness or just say "check your email".

Edge cases to resolve

Edge case 1 — same address searched again. Do they pay again and get a fresh report, get the cached one for free, or get a discounted re-run? This affects both the UX (do you show "report already exists" before checkout?) and the pipeline (do you cache reports or always regenerate?).

Edge case 2 — wrong address, already paid. There's no refund or correction flow anywhere in the sitemap. This needs either a grace period on the confirmation page ("wrong address? fix it before we generate") or a support contact path. Without it, this becomes a customer-service problem on day one.

Edge case 3 — similar but different addresses (e.g. 12 vs 21 Elm St). The address search on the home page needs a confirmation step — show the matched address and ask "is this the right property?" before the modal triggers. This is a UX pattern, not just an error state.

Market scope & timeline

Initial geographic focus

Counties with high sales volume and accessible public data:

Westchester County NYC Bergen County Maricopa (AZ) Texas counties Florida Las Vegas Orange County Seattle / Kings County

Data accessibility varies by county — some require subscriptions (Westchester is $1,200/year), while others have free public access.

Timeline

Revised MVP target: end of May (original end-of-April target no longer feasible). Budget is limited — the goal is to identify the true MVP scope to make the project financially viable, and to test market fit before investing heavily.

Potential pivot

If consumer adoption is slow, Knowsy could target real estate agents who need reports for their listings.
