Product Definition

Carfax for real estate — a single, structured report that surfaces everything a buyer needs to know about a home before they make an offer.

Status: Discovery Version 1.0 June 2026 Owner: Mitimiti Estudio

What is Knowsy?

Knowsy is a consumer-facing property research platform that generates comprehensive, AI-powered reports about any residential property before a buyer makes an offer. Think Carfax for real estate — a single, structured report that surfaces everything a buyer needs to know about a home: its ownership history, deed chain, tax record, neighborhood data, and potential red flags.

The core insight: homebuyers make the largest financial decision of their lives with surprisingly little structured information. Existing platforms like Zillow and Redfin focus on finding a home. Knowsy focuses on understanding one.

Problem statement

Today's homebuyer — particularly millennials and zillennials — faces a fragmented research process:

  • Property history is scattered across county clerk websites, tax portals, and title companies
  • Historical deed documents (some dating back to 1913) are scanned PDFs with no semantic search
  • Potential red flags (shared driveways, utility easements, estate ownership complications) are only discovered at closing — too late
  • Neighborhood intelligence (school ratings, noise levels, walkability, HOA info, town meeting minutes) lives across dozens of separate sources
  • Professional title searches are expensive and happen post-offer, not pre-offer

The gap: there is no consumer product that aggregates, interprets, and presents this data in an accessible, modern format before a buyer decides to make an offer.

Product vision

"Know before you offer."

Knowsy is the research layer of the home buying journey. It doesn't replace real estate agents or title companies — it empowers buyers to enter conversations and negotiations with full context.

Long-term vision: become the default pre-offer research tool for residential real estate in the US, starting with high-volume, data-accessible counties and expanding nationwide.

Target users

  • Digitally native, used to paying for SaaS tools that save time

Report structure (MVP)

Each Knowsy report covers six sections:

6.1 · Property Overview

Address, current assessed value, square footage, lot size, zoning, current owner, year built, last sale price and date.

Sources: Regrid (parcel data), county tax records, RealtyAPI

6.2 · Deed & Ownership History

Complete deed chain from current owner back to earliest available record. AI-extracted data from scanned documents including grantor/grantee, document type, liber/page references, recording date, and any encumbrances, easements, or restrictions noted.

Sources: Westchester County Clerk (WRO), OCR + vision model pipeline, Inngest workflow
This is the hardest and most defensible section. Competitor tools do not do this.

6.3 · Tax & Financial History

Annual assessed value history, tax amount history, mill rate, any delinquencies or tax liens.

Sources: County tax records, Regrid

6.4 · Neighborhood & Town Intelligence

School district ratings, walkability score, flood zone (FEMA NFHL), noise pollution score (HowLoud), HOA presence and documents, town meeting minutes referencing the property or area.

Sources: HowLoud API, Walk Score, FEMA NFHL, county meeting minutes scraping

6.5 · Social Sentiment Phase 2

Reddit and Nextdoor mentions of the neighborhood or street. Aggregated sentiment score with representative quotes.

Sources: GDELT, Reddit API, Nextdoor (TBD)

6.6 · Quality of Life Snapshot Phase 2

Top-rated restaurants, parks, and amenities within 1 mile. Aggregated from public review data.

Sources: Google Places API or similar

Technical architecture (summary)

Knowsy is built on a Next.js / TypeScript stack deployed on Vercel, with the following key layers:

LayerTechnology
FrontendNext.js 14 (RSC + Route Handlers)
DatabaseNeon (serverless Postgres) + JSONB caching for vendor payloads
Workflow orchestrationInngest (durable, retryable workflows)
File storageVercel Blob (deed PDFs, scanned docs)
OCR / AI extractionVision model pipeline (deed chain extraction)
Parcel dataRegrid API (authoritative county records)
Listing dataRealtyAPI
Deed documentsWestchester County Clerk (WRO) — subscription $1,200/yr

Two generation modes under evaluation:

  • Pre-scraped: Knowsy crawls all active MLS listings nightly → reports ready instantly
  • On-demand: Report generated at request time → 10–15 min generation window

MVP will likely use on-demand with a queued async model and email notification on completion.

Key differentiators

  • Deed chain AI extraction — No competitor automates the reading of historical deed documents. This is the hardest data problem in property research and Knowsy's primary moat.
  • Consumer-first design — Competitors (DataTree, PropertyShark, title plants) are built for professionals. Knowsy is built for a buyer who has never read a deed.
  • Pre-offer timing — Unlike title insurance (which runs post-offer), Knowsy gives buyers leverage before they commit.
  • Single source of truth — One URL, one report, everything a buyer needs. No tab-switching.

MVP scope

In scope

  • Address search input → report generation flow
  • Property overview (parcel + tax data via Regrid)
  • Deed chain extraction (Westchester County Clerk, AI pipeline)
  • Social sentiment (Reddit/Nextdoor)
  • Environmental section (flood zone + noise score)
  • Quality of life section (restaurants, parks)
  • Report display UI (web, desktop-first)
  • Stripe payment ($40/report)
  • Email delivery of report PDF
  • Westchester County, NY only

Out of scope (MVP)

  • Agent-facing product or B2B pricing
  • Mobile app
  • User accounts / report history
  • Multi-county coverage
  • Pre-scraped report inventory
