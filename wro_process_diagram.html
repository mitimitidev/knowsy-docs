How an automated title search works

From a single street address to a complete chain of ownership and a list of encumbrances — fully automated.

1Start with an address

Someone enters a property's street address — that's the only input needed.

Input · "985 Plymouth Street, Pelham"

2Look up the assessment roll

The address is matched against county assessment records to find who owns the property (the owner of record), plus the parcel ID. The county deed index can only be searched by owner name — so this step turns an address into a searchable name.

Result · owner of record + parcel

3Search the county land records (WRO)

The system signs in to Westchester County's online records portal and searches the public index for that owner — finding the deed by which they acquired the property.

Result · matching recorded deed

4Retrieve the deed document

The recorded deed — a scanned image of the original paper document — is downloaded from the county's archive.

Result · deed image (PDF)

5Read the document (OCR)

The scanned image is run through text recognition (OCR), turning the picture of the deed into machine-readable text — including the old typed and handwritten language.

Result · full deed text

6Understand the deed (AI analysis)

AI reads the text and pulls out the key facts: who sold to whom, the date, the legal description, and any easements, covenants, liens or red flags. Crucially, it also finds the reference to the previous deed — the link to the prior owner.

Result · parties · flags · prior-deed link
↺

Repeat — walk back through history

Using the prior-deed reference from step 6, the process loops back to step 3 and finds that earlier deed, then the one before it, and so on — stepping backward through every owner until the chain reaches as far as the records go.

7Deliver the title report

All the confirmed deeds are assembled into a single chain of title — the full ownership history — alongside the list of encumbrances and red flags found along the way, ready for review.

Output · chain of title + encumbrances

In plain terms

  • Address → owner. The assessment roll is the bridge: county deeds are filed by name, not address, so we look up the owner first.
  • Find & read. We pull the actual recorded deed and read it with OCR, then let AI extract the meaningful parts.
  • Follow the trail. Every deed points to the one before it — we follow that trail backward to rebuild the whole ownership history.
  • Hand off a report. The result is a clear chain of ownership plus any easements, liens, or concerns — to speed up, not replace, a professional title review.
